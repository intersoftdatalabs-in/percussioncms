Personalization API

Personalization (also known as P13N) is composed of three major Systems that follow a Service Oriented Architecture.
SystemDescription
Visitor TrackingSee {@link com.percussion.soln.p13n.tracking}
Content DeliverySee {@link com.percussion.soln.p13n.delivery}
SegmentationSee {@link com.percussion.soln.segment}

Very High Overview

Personalization works by associating segments or tags of a certain weight with visitors and then by selectively displaying content based on those weights.

This is done in two steps when a visitor visits a web page:

  1. Track the visitor by setting segment weights associated with the page onto the visitor. ({@link com.percussion.soln.p13n.tracking Tracking})
  2. Portions of the page are filled through dynamic list of HTML snippets whose contents get filtered based on the segment weights. ({@link com.percussion.soln.p13n.delivery Delivery})
Some pages may only do one of those two steps.

Example of personalization

Imagine a new visitor visiting page A that is tagged with segments "US" and "Customer". The visitor will have a profile like:
Segment IdentifierWeight
US1
Customer1

If page A has a dynamic list with three snippets:

SnippetSegments
IInternational,Contractor
JUS,Contractor
KUS,Customer
and the list has a maximum size of 2 and minimum size of 1.

The list will be displayed for the visitor as:

SnippetScore
K2
J1

Content Contributors Control Personalization

Content contributors can actively control personalization of dynamic lists by setting properties on the list and by associating segments with snippets in the list and by associating segments with landing pages.

Segmenting landing pages will cause visitors to have their profile updated to the matching segments usually by increasing it by one. This update is known as the "update" tracking action.
